Locally Redundant Storage (LRS):
Synchronously makes 3 copies of your data within a single physical data center in your primary region. 
It is the most cost-effective option

Zone-Redundant Storage (ZRS):
Synchronously replicates your data across 3 distinct Availability Zones (separate data centers) within the same primary region. 
It provides higher availability and protects against full data center outages.

Geo-Redundant Storage (GRS):
Combines LRS with asynchronous replication to a secondary region. It keeps 6 copies of your data in total—3 in your primary region and 3 in a paired secondary region, 
hundreds of miles away. It protects against region-wide disasters
